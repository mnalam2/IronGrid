02 Approach

The IronGrid method.

Contracting has a reputation problem. We fix it with a process that is boring in the best possible way: predictable, transparent, and documented.

  1. 01

    Scope

    A short discovery call to understand the problem, the constraints, and what “done” looks like. You get a written proposal with a timeline and a price — not a vague estimate.

  2. 02

    Staff

    You meet the actual engineers who will do the work before anything is signed. The people in the proposal are the people in your repos. No bait and switch, ever.

  3. 03

    Ship

    We work in your repositories, your tracker, and your process. Weekly demos of running software and honest status reports — including the parts that aren't going well.

  4. 04

    Support

    Every engagement ends with documentation and a real handoff. Your team can run what we built. If you want us to stick around, retainers are available — but never required.

03 Engagement models

Three ways to work with us.

Simple contract structures under a standard MSA and SOW. Pick the shape that fits — or tell us what you need and we'll propose one.

Project-Based

Fixed scope · fixed price

A defined deliverable with milestones, acceptance criteria, and a price agreed up front. Scope changes are written down and re-priced — no surprise invoices.

  • Written SOW with milestones
  • Weekly demos of running software
  • Documentation & handoff included

Best for: a well-defined build or migration

Most common

Staff Augmentation

Monthly · per engineer

Senior contract engineers embedded in your team on a simple monthly basis. Scale up when the roadmap demands it, scale down when it doesn't.

  • Vetted seniors, interviewed by you
  • Your repos, your process, your standup
  • 30-day flexibility — no long lock-ins

Best for: capacity gaps and deadline crunches

Retained Partnership

Ongoing · advisory + build

A standing engineering partner for companies without a full-time technical team — architecture ownership, vendor management, and hands-on development as needed.

  • Reserved monthly capacity
  • Architecture & roadmap ownership
  • Priority response times

Best for: long-term product evolution
