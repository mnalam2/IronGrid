FAQ

Questions, answered straight.

How long does a website take?

A launch page can go live in about a week. Most business websites take two to four weeks from kickoff to launch, depending on the number of pages and how quickly content comes together. Stores and custom builds vary — your quote always includes a timeline.

How much does a website cost?

It depends on the size and features, which is why every project gets a flat written quote before anything starts — no hourly billing, no surprise line items. Tell us what you need and you'll have a real number within a business day, free, with zero obligation.

Do I need to write the content myself?

No. Bring what you have — even if that's just a logo and a phone number — and we'll help shape the words, structure the pages, and source imagery. You review and approve everything before it goes live.

What about domains, hosting, and email?

We set all of it up — domain, hosting, business email, analytics — in accounts that belong to you, and connect everything so it just works. If you're on a care plan we manage it day to day; either way, you hold the keys.

Can you fix or redesign my current site?

Yes — redesigns are a big part of what we do. We keep what's working, fix what isn't, migrate your content, and make sure your Google ranking survives the move. If your current site just needs small fixes rather than a rebuild, we'll tell you that honestly.

What happens after launch?

The site is yours. Many clients add a care plan — updates, content edits, backups, security, and a real human answering support emails. Others take the launch walkthrough and run it themselves. Both are completely fine, and you can switch anytime.
